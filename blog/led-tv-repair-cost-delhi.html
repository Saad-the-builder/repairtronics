What Affects the Cost of TV Repair?

1. TV Screen Size

A 32-inch TV will naturally cost less to repair than a 55-inch or 65-inch TV. Larger screens use bigger backlights, larger panels, and more powerful power supply boards. For example, backlight repair on a 32-inch TV might cost Rs 1,500, but the same repair on a 55-inch TV could go up to Rs 3,500–4,000.

2. TV Brand

Some brands like Samsung, LG, and Sony use parts that are readily available in Delhi markets, which keeps costs lower. But lesser-known Chinese brands or premium models from brands like Panasonic or Philips sometimes need parts that have to be ordered, adding to both cost and wait time.

3. Type of Problem

Not all repairs are equal. A simple power supply capacitor replacement might cost only Rs 800, while a full panel replacement on a large TV could be Rs 8,000 or more. The good news is that most TV problems are fixable at a low cost — you do not always need an expensive replacement.

4. Part Availability

If the part your TV needs is available locally in Lajpat Nagar or Palika Bazaar electronics markets, you pay less. If it needs to be ordered from another city or from abroad, it adds shipping cost and wait time. We always try to source parts locally first to keep your bill low.

Let Us Explain Each Repair Type

Backlight Repair (Rs 1,500 – 4,000)

This is the most common repair we do. If your TV screen is completely dark but you can still hear sound, or if you see a very faint picture when you shine a torch on the screen, the backlight LEDs have failed. We open the panel, replace the damaged LED strips, and your TV works like new. This repair is much cheaper than buying a new TV and takes just a few hours.

Panel Replacement (Rs 3,000 – 8,000)

If your TV screen has cracks, coloured lines running across it, or large dark patches, the panel itself is damaged. Panel replacement is the most expensive repair because the panel is the most costly part of any TV. However, even this is usually cheaper than buying a brand new television. We source good quality replacement panels that come with a warranty.

Motherboard Repair (Rs 1,500 – 3,500)

If your TV is not turning on at all, has no display, keeps restarting on its own, or the smart TV features have stopped working, the motherboard could be the issue. Sometimes a few components on the board fail and can be replaced individually, which costs less. If the entire board needs replacement, it costs a bit more.

Power Supply Repair (Rs 800 – 2,000)

Power supply problems are common and affordable to fix. If your TV turns off by itself, does not turn on, or the standby light blinks, the power supply board likely has failed capacitors or a blown fuse. This is usually a quick and inexpensive repair.

How to Avoid Getting Overcharged for TV Repair

Unfortunately, some repair shops in Delhi take advantage of customers who do not know much about electronics. Here are some tips to protect yourself:

  • Always ask for a diagnosis first. A good repair shop will tell you the exact problem and quote a price before starting any work. If someone starts repairing without telling you the cost, walk away.
  • Get the old parts back. Ask the technician to show you the damaged part and return it to you. This prevents the common scam of charging for part replacement when only a minor fix was needed.
  • Compare quotes. If a quote feels too high, get a second opinion. But remember — the cheapest option is not always the best. Low-quality parts can cause the same problem again in a few months.
  • Ask about warranty. Any reputable repair service should give you a warranty on both parts and labour. At Repair Tronics, we provide a warranty on every repair we do.
  • Check reviews. Before handing over your TV to anyone, check their Google reviews and ratings. Real customer feedback tells you a lot.
Pro Tip: If a repair shop tells you that your TV panel is damaged without even opening the TV, be cautious. Many problems that look like panel issues are actually backlight or motherboard problems that cost much less to fix.
